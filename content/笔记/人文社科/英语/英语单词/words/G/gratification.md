# 词义
- 英：/ˌɡrætɪfɪˈkeɪʃn/； 美：/ˌɡrætɪfɪˈkeɪʃn/
- #词性/n  满足；满意；快感；令人喜悦的事物
# 例句
- A feed will usually provide instant gratification to a crying baby .
	- 喂食通常可使正在哭闹的婴儿立即得到满足。
- The assumption is that children shouldn 't chat in the classroom because it hinders hard work ； instead , they should learn to delay gratification so that they can pursue abstract goals , like going to college .
	- 假设孩子们不应该在教室里聊天,因为这会妨碍他们努力学习；相反,他们应该学会延迟满足感,这样他们就可以追求抽象的目标,比如上大学。
- ' Gratification ' and ' gratitude ' have the same etymological root .
	- gratification和gratitude的词源相同。
